# Expert Selection Scorecard

## Purpose

This scorecard prevents the research corpus from becoming a list of popular creators. Each selected expert must demonstrate practical relevance to organic LinkedIn strategy for B2B SaaS.

## Evaluation criteria

| Criterion | Weight | What qualifies |
|---|---:|---|
| Direct B2B SaaS operating experience | 30 | Built, led, or materially influenced marketing, growth, content, or category strategy inside a B2B SaaS company. |
| Organic LinkedIn relevance | 20 | Publishes practical LinkedIn content on buyer education, demand creation, messaging, distribution, or operator-led content. |
| Primary-source depth | 15 | Has public long-form material such as interviews, podcasts, talks, or YouTube videos that explain their reasoning. |
| Practical specificity | 15 | Shares frameworks, experiments, decisions, trade-offs, or results rather than generic advice. |
| Recency and activity | 10 | Has relevant public material from the last 12 months when possible. |
| Perspective diversity | 10 | Adds a distinct point of view: founder-led content, demand generation, distribution, messaging, category creation, or full-funnel strategy. |

**Maximum score:** 100

## Minimum selection bar

An expert should score at least **75/100** and meet both non-negotiable requirements:

1. Direct B2B SaaS operating experience.
2. At least one recent LinkedIn artifact and one long-form source suitable for transcript collection.

## Exclusion criteria

Do not select an expert solely because they:

- have a large audience;
- sell generic LinkedIn ghostwriting services;
- publish motivational or engagement-bait content;
- provide only high-level marketing advice;
- cannot be connected to B2B SaaS practice;
- have no usable primary-source material.

## Evidence handling

A score is provisional until the source links are manually verified.

LinkedIn post collection remains manual. YouTube and podcast transcript collection will be automated later through the project pipeline.
